Treehouse is an affordable alternative for a lot of people who want to get jobs in tech and can't afford formal university degrees. I'm very aware of this need: since last year, a team of volunteers and I have used e-learning and on-site mentorship to give refugees in Berlin the opportunity to get hired and integrate quickly.

It's clear that bringing quality technology education to people from all walks of life will make the world a more open and happy place. Here are two ways to work on this goal.


Tend to the needs of students everywhere

As more and more students come to Treehouse for acquiring technology skills, it's important to have an accurate picture of their backgrounds, wants and needs. There's an opportunity to develop and maintain personas that reflect distinct user archetypes, and connect with local communities of technology learners.

Basic programming courses in Spanish

The US is on track to be the #1 Spanish-speaking country in the world. While the best jobs in software design and engineering demand fluent English, programming courses in Spanish would spark the motivation of the hispanic community to integrate in the digital economy. They'll have one more reason to learn good English once the coding bug bites them.
