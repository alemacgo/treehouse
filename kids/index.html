CS basics course

Using Swift Playgrounds, for kids aged 8-13

Computer science basics are as essential as math, but they're usually not taught in US schools.

  • Goals
  •  Provide a guided, visual, fun introduction to computing for kids aged 8-13
  •  Be one of the first companies to leverage Playgrounds for teaching
  •  Generate quality content for the Hour of Code movement
  •  Encourage Treehouse students who are parents to introduce their kids to computer science
  • Measuring success
  •  Use analytics to gauge course popularity and engagement
  •  Conduct follow-up interviews to assess the kids' interest in computer science after taking the course

Playgrounds are a great medium for teaching computer science fundamentals. They can be used to teach programming with LOGO, as Daniel Steinberg shows with his Turtle playground.

People learn easily when they have concrete, familiar objects to think with. Playgrounds allow us to quickly create and modify visual elements. Here's an example of how we can teach the basics of object-oriented programming while making a holiday card.

Since Playgrounds are live-coding environments, they make dynamic concepts like loops much easier to teach. People seldom get anything exactly right the first time: programmers spend their time making tiny course corrections to approximate a solution to a problem, something we call debugging. This is a way of thinking Treehouse should enable and encourage in young students.

We could teach kids fundamental technology concepts beyond programming, like binary numbers and how machines represent data. Immediate feedback is key here: when the student replaces a 0 for a 1, she will immediately see the result without dealing with the typical read-eval-print loop.
