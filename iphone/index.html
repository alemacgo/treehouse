A Treehouse iPhone app

Students can use commutes and short breaks to quickly brush up on past lessons.

  • Goals
  •  Enable students to review their knowledge on the go
  •  Leverage spaced repetition techniques to help students retain information
  •  Getting free marketing when people see students use the Treehouse app on their phones
  • Measuring success
  •  Use analytics to track installations and engagement with push notifications
  •  Keep track of App Store reviews
  •  Study the long-term performance of students who use the iPhone app

I understand Treehouse's decision to release and maintain an iPad-only app: the video lectures and coding exercises only make sense on a bigger screen. I couldn't help but notice, however, that iPhone apps for learning how to code are trending on the App Store. This made me think of ways Treehouse could serve its students with a companion iPhone app.

Apps like Swifty and Lrn seem to be thriving just by packaging introductory programming exercises in a mobile-friendly interface. What if Treehouse offered a refresher app that allowed students to re-take quizzes of past lessons in their spare time?

We could even take advantage of the iPhone's notifications and spaced repetition techniques to boost students' retention of difficult technical concepts or tricky language syntax.

I think we can design and develop such an app well in a matter of weeks. Let's ride the wave of code-teaching phone apps. We may even get featured by Apple, a happy event that would come with an influx of new Treehouse students! ðŸŽ‰
