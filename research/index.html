Workspaces are great at simulating a real professional programming environment, and lowering the complexity barriers of proper configuration, versions management, and command-line usage.

There's an opportunity to create the next generation of programming learning environments, where the goal wouldn't be to mimic current tools that programmers work with every day, but to support the understanding of essential concepts that aren't easy to visualize. Here are some ideas.

Shortening the feedback loop

When I'm working on a website, I use LiveReload to avoid the hassle of refreshing the page to see my changes. This shortened feedback loop is useful for experienced programmers, and I'd argue it's essential for newcomers that want to make fast progress. Codepen, despite not being geared specifically for learning, understands this and has developed a great product with immediate updates. On Treehouse's playgrounds, immediate updates would free the learner's working memory, resulting in increased focus and enhanced understanding.

Showing the data and the system status

Concepts like getting data from a remote server are tricky to grasp at first. Prasan does a great job at explaining this in his beginner iOS course, and Workspaces could further support understanding by allowing students to visualize the data involved, the status of the client and the server, and the interactions between them.
